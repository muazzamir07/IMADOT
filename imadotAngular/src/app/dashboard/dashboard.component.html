Lung Cancer
An estimated 1.9 million new cancer cases were diagnosed in 2021 in the United States and more than 600k people died from cancer the same year- making cancer a worldwide conundrum. Lungs cancer is no exception with about 236,740 cases in 2022 in the United States. However, in most cases, lung cancer is not diagnosed at an early stage which eventually risks the patient’s life as chances of survival are reduced.


This issue becomes worst in the developing countries like Pakistan where the doctors, radiologists, and equipments are not readily available. In such situations medical image analysis serves a vital role for diagnosis of lung cancer.
Recently many approaches based on AI, ML, and DL have been used for automatic detection and segmentation of lung tumors. This helps in reducing the doctors’ involvement and time taken for manual detection of tumors thus providing more people an opportunity to beat cancer in the early stages.
Why IMADOT?
Existing attempts at lung tumor segmentation are based mainly on the features extracted from the CT scans. IMADOT provides a multimodal approach that performs feature fusion on CT and PET scans. The reason of combining two modalities is to make use of the prodigious benefits of both scans at the same time.

Moreover, the 3D visualization will assist the doctors in analysis of tumors and surrounding cells and tissues. This will provide anatomical information that is difficult to obtain from the traditional methods. This will also help in improving the accuracy and decision making process in radiotherapy.
